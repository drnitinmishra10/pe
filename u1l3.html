📖 Introduction to Generative AI

Definition:
Generative AI is a category of Artificial Intelligence that creates new content such as text, images, audio, video, code, and designs.

Unlike traditional AI, which analyzes existing data, Generative AI produces entirely new content based on patterns learned from huge datasets.

⚡ Traditional AI vs Generative AI

Traditional AI Generative AI
Analyzes Data Creates Data
Classifies Objects Generates Objects
Predicts Outcomes Creates New Outputs
Detects Fraud Writes Reports
Recognizes Images Generates Images

🧠 How Generative AI Works

Large Dataset ↓ Training ↓ AI Model ↓ Prompt ↓ Generated Output

Step 1: Training

AI models are trained using billions of examples from books, websites, images, videos, and other sources.

Step 2: Pattern Learning

The model learns relationships, patterns, and structures in data.

Step 3: Prompt Processing

A user provides instructions known as a Prompt.

Step 4: Content Generation

The AI generates content based on the prompt and learned patterns.

💬 Understanding Prompts

Example 1: Weak Prompt

Write about AI.

Example 2: Better Prompt

Act as a professor. Explain Artificial Intelligence in 150 words with examples for first-year engineering students.

Better prompts generate better outputs. This concept forms the foundation of Prompt Engineering.

✅ Advantages of Generative AI

  • Increases productivity
  • Supports creativity
  • Generates content quickly
  • Automates repetitive tasks
  • Assists learning and research
  • Supports software development

⚠️ Limitations of Generative AI

  • May generate incorrect information
  • Can produce biased responses
  • Privacy concerns
  • Copyright issues
  • Requires human verification
  • Can be misused for misinformation
